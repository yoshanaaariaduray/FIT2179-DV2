Australia's Silent Crisis

Understanding Mental Health Across the Nation

4.3M Australians with a mental disorder
$13.8B Spent on mental health 2023-24
45% Ever seek help

Mental illness is one of Australia's most pressing health challenges. Yet despite billions spent on services, millions of Australians suffer in silence. This visualisation explores who is affected, what they experience, and whether help is truly reaching those who need it.

Where in Australia Are People Struggling Most?

Mental health disorders don't affect all Australians equally. Some states and territories report significantly higher rates than others but the rate alone doesn't tell the full story.

The Northern Territory has the highest disorder rate at 28.8% but with only 137,600 people, NSW's lower rate of 19.5% means far more Australians are actually affected there. Hover over a state to see the details.

NT's rate appears alarming but its confidence interval spans from 15.2% to 42.4%, meaning the true rate could be almost anywhere in that range. NSW's estimate is far more reliable with a narrow range of 17.9% to 21.0%.

Who Is Most at Risk?

Mental illness does not discriminate but it does disproportionately affect certain groups.

Young Australians aged 16-24 have nearly double the national average rate of 21.5%.

Females experience higher rates across all disorder types except substance use where males are more than twice as likely to be affected.

What Are Australians Facing?

Not all mental disorders are the same. Understanding what Australians are actually experiencing is crucial to understanding the scale of the crisis.

Anxiety is by far the most common disorder affecting 1 in 6 Australians aged 16-85.

Social Phobia and PTSD are the most prevalent anxiety subtypes females consistently experience higher rates.

Young people aged 16-24 dominate anxiety disorders while substance use peaks in early adulthood.

Millions Are Suffering in Silence

Of every 100 Australians with a mental disorder only 45 sought professional help.

Men are even less likely to seek help only 36 in 100 consulted a health professional.

Medicare mental health visits surged during COVID-19 but have since declined raising questions about whether demand is truly being met.

Mental health services are overwhelmingly concentrated in major cities leaving rural and remote Australians severely underserved.

Are We Spending Enough?

Australia has significantly increased its investment in mental health over the past decade. But is the money reaching the right places?

Government mental health spending has grown by 70% over the past decade yet demand continues to outpace supply.

NT receives $128 more per person than NSW raising serious questions about equity in mental health funding.

What Needs to Change?

Young People

Need more targeted support and early intervention programs

Rural Access

Mental health services must expand beyond major cities urgently

Men's Health

Men need to be encouraged and supported to seek help

Australia's mental health crisis is real, widespread, and unevenly distributed. While investment is growing, the gap between those who need help and those who receive it remains alarming particularly for young people, men, and those living outside major cities.
